The Employment and Labour Relations Court (ELRC) has powers to take Administrative actions to achieve efficient disposition of multiple employment suits.

In a ruling delivered on 19th September 2025, we successfully opposed an Appeal at the Court of Appeal wherein the employer had challenged a decision of the ELRC to determine multiple claims through a test suit.

At the High Court, we argued that the seventeen claims of our Clients raised similar issues which would easily be dispensed with by way of a test suit as provided for under the Employment Act which position the learned Judge concured with.

Dissatisfied by this finding, the employer moved to the Court of Appeal seeking stay of proceedings pending an intended Appeal.

The Appellate Court held that where a Court takes an administrative action that benefits both parties, it cannot be viewed as impinging on the right to a fair trial. Consequently, the employer was found not to have established an arguable appeal or how the same will rendered nugatory, absent stay.
